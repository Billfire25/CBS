Common Chimney Questions

Chimneys should usually be cleaned at least once a year, especially if you use your fireplace often. Regular cleaning removes soot and creosote buildup that can block airflow and increase fire risks, helping your chimney work safely and efficiently throughout the heating season.

Signs of chimney damage include cracked bricks, crumbling mortar, water leaks, rusted components, and smoke backing into your home. You might also notice strange odors or poor fireplace performance. Addressing these issues early helps prevent more expensive structural repairs later.

Creosote is a highly flammable substance that forms when wood burns. If it builds up inside your chimney, it can ignite and cause a dangerous chimney fire. Professional cleaning removes this material, improving ventilation and reducing serious fire hazards inside your home.
